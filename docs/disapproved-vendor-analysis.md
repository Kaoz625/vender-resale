# DOCCS Disapproved Vendor List — Full Analysis
**Source:** NYS DOCCS Incarcerated Individual Disapproved Package Vendor List (Updated 3/12/2026)  
**Purpose:** Understand why vendors get banned so we avoid every single trigger.

---

## How the Disapproval System Works

There is **no approval process** — any vendor not on the disapproved list can operate. You ship until you're banned.

**The only documented trigger (Directive 4911, Section IV-F-1):**
> "If a vendor attempts to transmit contraband within a package, such matters must be reported by the Deputy Superintendent for Administration (DSA) to Support Operations via Form SOPVDR, 'Incarcerated Individual Package Vendor Disapproval Request Form,' to request addition to the disapproved vendor list."

Key facts:
- Any DSA at any facility can file a SOPVDR against you
- **No warning. No appeal process.** One form and you're done.
- No reasons are ever published — the list only shows name, address, and date added
- DOCCS does periodic batch sweeps (multiple vendors banned same day — see date patterns below)

## Why Vendors Get Banned — Categories

### 1. Contraband in Packages (The Core Trigger)
A single package with contraband = SOPVDR form = permanent ban. No warnings.

**Most clear-cut example:** Bug Spray on Paper (2/14/2024) — the name literally describes drug-soaked paper (synthetic cannabinoids sprayed on paper, dried, mailed as printed material). This is the most brazen entry on the entire list.

**Re-Nu Shoe Repair** (12/2/2003, the oldest ban) — repaired shoes returned with contraband hidden inside.

### 2. Non-Compliant Food
Directive 4911 food rules are extremely strict:
- Must be **commercially or hermetically sealed** — no bakery, restaurant, deli, or home-prepared food
- No hot peppers, marshmallows, energy drinks
- No USDA surplus or government commodity items
- No fresh produce, meat, dairy, or eggs

**Banned food vendors:** Bakery HNY, Bueno Snackz, Crazy Snackz, Freedom Foods & Goods, KRM Kollel Supermarket, Nana's Soul Kitchen, NY Food Feast

### 3. Non-Compliant Footwear
Shoes must have NO:
- Hollow or platform heels/soles (hiding spots)
- Air/pump inflation chambers (Nike Air, etc.)
- Memory foam
- Metal shanks, metal or stone ornamentation
- Pockets, compartments, or zippers
- Pointed toes, spiked heels
- Camouflage pattern

**Banned footwear vendors:** Re-Nu Shoe Repair, SHOES4U, Sneaker Stash, Underground Kicks & Accessories

### 4. Media / Entertainment Vendors
A striking number of music and entertainment vendors are banned. Likely causes:
- CDs/cassettes/DVDs can conceal contraband inside cases
- Selling bootleg/unlicensed media
- Media formats not on the Directive 4911 allowable list
- Multiple music vendors added in batches (3/23/2015, 10/3/2018) = periodic sweeps targeting this category

**Banned media vendors:** Audio Officialz, Cassette Kingz, Foreign Entertainment, Grindz Entertainment, M&G Outlet, Madison Avenue Entertainment Group, Mainline Music Distributors, Sounds Good Music LLC, Uptown Entertainment

### 5. Brand Names That Flag Security
DOCCS officers open every package. A brand name that reads like a threat or references criminal activity draws maximum scrutiny:
- **Bombmissary** — "bomb" in a package vendor name
- **Bug Spray on Paper** — describes contraband method
- **Born Greedy**, **FelonSpace.com** — criminal identity branding
- **Nice & Naughty** — adult content implication

### 6. Services Disguised as Packages
Routing legal or administrative services through the package system instead of the legal mail channel:
- **Prisoner Legal Help (Legal Associates LLP)** — legal services through package room
- **TFL Administrative Services** — administrative services through package room

### 7. Repackagers / Non-Product Vendors
**EAJ Packaging** (Merrick, NY) — a packaging company that was likely acting as a pass-through, obscuring the true origin of contents. Added November 2022, right after the May 2022 policy change.

### 8. Tobacco Without NY State Tax Stamps
**Moises Cigar Shop** — tobacco is allowed but must have NY State Tax Stamps on every pack. No exceptions. Most likely sent cigars without stamps.

### 9. Botanical / Herbal Products
**Original Products Botannical** — botanica shop selling herbs, incense, oils. Loose plant materials are hard to screen and some can be used to make intoxicants. Items likely not on Directive 4911 Attachment A.

### 10. Oversized General Catalogs
**Heartland America** (Chaska, MN) — large national discount catalog. Sells thousands of items, most not on the Directive 4911 allowable list. Unable or unwilling to filter their catalog to compliant items.

## What Keeps You Off the List

### The Non-Negotiables
1. **Only sell items on Directive 4911 Attachment A.** If it's not explicitly listed as allowed, do not sell it.
2. **Printed return address on every package.** Handwritten = automatic x-ray. Never handwrite anything.
3. **Commercially sealed food only.** No exceptions. No bakery items, no hot peppers, no energy drinks.
4. **Footwear compliance.** No hollow soles, no air chambers, no memory foam, no compartments.
5. **NY State Tax Stamps on all tobacco.** No exceptions.
6. **Never act as a repackager.** You must be the actual vendor of the products you ship.

### Risk Management
- Keep a log of every package shipped (date, facility, DIN, contents, tracking number)
- If a package is rejected, find out why immediately and fix the issue
- Do not sell across too many categories before you know the rules cold — pick 2-3 categories and master compliance in those
- Do not use brand names, product names, or marketing copy that references crime, drugs, weapons, or adult content

### Your Business Name
"Vender Resale" is clean — no red flags. Good.
