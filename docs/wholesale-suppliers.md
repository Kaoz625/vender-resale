## Startup Sourcing Sequence

### Phase 1 — Week 1-2 (Start immediately, no accounts needed)
- **Sam's Club Plus** ($110/year) — bulk food, hygiene, snacks. 15–25% below retail MSRP. Use to test SKUs before committing to wholesale cases.
- **Costco Business Center** (free business membership) — Kirkland Signature tuna, trail mix, nuts, jerky, soap/shampoo cases. 20–30% margins — thin but workable at launch.

### Phase 2 — Week 2-4 (Apply for wholesale accounts)
1. **Shop the King** (shoptheking.us) — snacks + meats, 1–2 day account approval
2. **S&S Activewear** (ssactivewear.com) — blank apparel, requires business license + state tax forms
3. **DollarDays** (dollardays.com) — hygiene, low minimums, accessible for startups
4. **Holmdel Footwear** (holmdelfootwear.net) — complete Wholesale Account Form

### Phase 3 — Month 2+
- **McLane Company via McLaneXpress** (mclaneco.com) — nation's #1 convenience store distributor, 50,000+ SKUs. $500–1,000 minimum/delivery for independents. Real wholesale tier: 20–35% below retail.

### Phase 4 — Month 3+
- **KeHE Distributors** (kehe.com/work-with-kehe) — natural/specialty foods, requires retailer account
- **Ingram Content Group** (ingramcontent.com) — books, 40–55% off list price (Baker & Taylor filed Chapter 11 March 2026 — Ingram is now the only major book wholesaler)

## Category 7: Books & Educational Materials

### Ingram Content Group (ingramcontent.com)
- **Now the dominant US book wholesaler** (Baker & Taylor filed Chapter 11, March 2026)
- Retailer discount: 40–55% off list price
- $20 list-price book costs ~$9–12 wholesale
- Standard account requires $50K+ sales history; startups use IngramSpark or Independent Retailer program

### Better World Books (betterworldbooks.com)
- Used books at steep discounts; bulk programs for resellers
- A $15 retail book sourced used at $2–4; excellent margins
- Good for: Dummies series, accounting, self-help, GED prep — exactly what families order
